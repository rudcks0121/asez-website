A new ASEZ research note argues that forests have crossed a threshold:
they're no longer only an environmental concern, but a measurable
component of national competitiveness in an era of climate volatility.

The note synthesizes findings from the World Bank, IPCC, and our own
field data across 28 chapters. Three core claims:

**1. Carbon balance is becoming a tradeable asset.** Countries with
   net-positive forest cover will increasingly benefit from
   international carbon markets.

**2. Climate resilience drives investment flows.** Multinationals are
   factoring forest density into siting decisions for new facilities.

**3. Youth-led restoration is fastest.** Programs designed and run by
   under-30 volunteers consistently outperform older models on cost
   per hectare and 5-year survival rates.
