For seven days in March, fifty-plus ASEZ chapters across Asia and the
Americas ran a coordinated reusable-cup challenge: every coffee, every
juice, every bubble tea — in a reusable cup or not at all.

The aggregated results, just released:

- **172,000 single-use cups avoided** across participating campuses
- **41% of campus cafes** offered a discount for reusable cups (up
  from 14% before the campaign)
- **3 universities** announced single-use cup bans for the next
  academic year

The campaign also generated something less measurable: a habit. Two
months after the official campaign ended, the proportion of students
carrying reusable cups remained more than double the pre-campaign
baseline.

Convenience is a default, not a choice — until someone makes the new
default the convenient one.
