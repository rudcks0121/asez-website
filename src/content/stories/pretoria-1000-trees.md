On a clear March morning in 2026, ASEZ chapters across South Africa
gathered for one of the most ambitious single-day tree-planting actions
in our history.

Volunteers planted 100 trees across nine sites in Pretoria — schools,
public parks, and community grounds — as part of the **Restore the
Earth** initiative running toward a target of 1,000 trees in the
region by the end of the year.

The Choco-e Elementary School site became the symbolic centerpiece.
Children helped place saplings into the ground while ASEZ volunteers
explained the role each tree will play in cooling local air, sheltering
wildlife, and capturing carbon over the next half-century.

> "This is not just about planting today. It's about who will sit
> under the shade of this tree in fifty years — and what world we
> hand them."
> — ASEZ chapter coordinator, Pretoria
