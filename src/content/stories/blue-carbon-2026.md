The **Blue Carbon Movement** entered its biggest year yet in 2026,
with active field projects in seven coastal countries: Indonesia,
the Philippines, Kenya, Brazil, India, Australia, and South Korea.

The 2026 priorities:

**Mangrove restoration** at six sites across Southeast Asia. Mangroves
store up to ten times more carbon per hectare than terrestrial forests,
and protect coastal communities from storm surge.

**Seagrass mapping** along the East African coast in partnership with
local universities and the UN Environment Programme.

**Tidal marsh advocacy** at the UN INC negotiations, where ASEZ is
pushing for formal recognition of Blue Carbon ecosystems in national
climate accounting.
