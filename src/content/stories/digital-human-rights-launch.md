ASEZ launched its first dedicated **Digital Human Rights** campaign
across twelve Asian countries this December, marking a new pillar of
work alongside our established environmental and humanitarian programs.

The campaign centers on three frontline issues for university students:

- **Privacy and consent** in campus learning platforms and surveillance
- **Mental health** impact of social-media exposure and recommendation
  systems
- **Equitable access** to digital tools across socioeconomic lines

The kickoff events featured public forums in Seoul, Manila, Jakarta,
and Hanoi — each pairing policy experts with student panels. A 12-country
survey, conducted in partnership with regional research universities,
will publish its first findings in early 2026.

Digital rights doesn't replace any of our existing work. It joins it.
