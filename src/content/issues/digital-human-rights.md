## Three frontline issues

- **Privacy and consent** in campus learning platforms, attendance
  surveillance, and proctoring tools
- **Mental health impact** of social-media recommendation systems,
  particularly on adolescents and young adults
- **Equitable access** to digital tools across socioeconomic lines —
  no student left behind because of broadband or device

## How we're working

The 2026 campaign launched across twelve Asian countries with
public forums pairing policy experts with student panels. A
12-country survey, conducted in partnership with regional research
universities, will publish its first findings later this year.

## Why ASEZ takes this on

Digital rights doesn't replace our environmental or humanitarian
work — it joins it. The same generation organizing the climate
movement is the one most affected by digital harms, and best
positioned to redesign the systems.
