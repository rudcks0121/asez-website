## Two timelines

**Hours and days — rapid response.** When disaster strikes, ASEZ
regional networks activate. Local chapters lead on-the-ground
support: shelter assistance, supply distribution, communication
relays, and connecting affected families with services.

**Months and years — long-term recovery.** The hard work begins after
the cameras leave. Rebuilding schools, restoring local economies,
mental-health support, and helping communities prepare for the next
shock.

## Where we've worked

ASEZ disaster-relief teams have responded to floods, earthquakes, and
typhoons across the Asia-Pacific, Latin America, and East Africa.
Each response is logged, analyzed, and the lessons folded into the
next deployment.

## Why students do this well

Student volunteers are mobile, networked, and unafraid of long hours.
They also bring something formal aid often misses: peers from the
affected community itself, ready to help rebuild what they know best.
